From Atlanta to Memphis
Travel through Civil Rights history
On January 20, the United States honors Dr. Martin Luther King Jr,  one of the most famous Civil Rights activists in American history. It’s one thing to read about Dr. King’s legacy in the south — it’s another to bear witness in person. Read on to learn more about the experiences that will make a trip through Georgia, Alabama, and Tennessee all the richer.

Visit MLK’s birthplace
This historic tour will take you through the Atlanta streets where Martin Luther King Jr. lived, worked, and preached. There’s no better way to learn about the Civil Rights Movement than from a local tour guide.

Explore Atlanta by trolley
Tour the southern metropolis on a cute trolley bus and roll past the most popular and historic parts of downtown Atlanta. From historic landmarks to modern must-sees, this 1.5-hour ride will show you the city in a new light.

Head to Birmingham
On your way to Memphis, make a stop in Birmingham. On this 3-hour tour, your knowledgeable guide will take you to historic spots in the city where ordinary people joined together to end segregation.

Experience Memphis’ history
Before you slip your blue suede shoes on, invest in a Heritage Pass. This handy ticket will give you access to essential cultural attractions like the National Civil Rights Museum, Blues Hall of Fame, and more.

Groove around Graceland
