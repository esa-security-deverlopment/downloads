CC Licenses in Action

                            

Paris Musées Releases 100,000+ Works Into the Public Domain 

 

The Paris Musées recently released more than 100,000 works under Creative Commons Zero (CCØ), putting the works into the public domain. This announcement is a cause for celebration as it marks the continuation of a growing trend among GLAM institutions, including the Metropolitan Museum of Art and the Cleveland Museum of Art, that are recognizing the importance of open access to artistic and cultural artifacts.

 

Access the collection! 

Creative Commons and USAID Collaborate on Guide to Open Licensing 

 

We collaborated with USAID and the Global Reading Network to co-publish "Open Licensing of Primary Grade Reading Materials: Consierations and Recommendations," a guide to open licenses that includes an introduction to the basics of copyright, an overview of the benefits of open licesning, and suggestions for implementing open licenses.

Get the guide

Over 300,000 Free Downloadable 3D Models Are Available on Sketchfab!

 

Sketchfab recently announced that over 300,000 free downloadable 3D models are available on their platform.

 

These models have been shared under a variety of CC Licenses by "artists, designers, culture organizations, and brands from around the world."
